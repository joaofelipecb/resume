Employment History

Programmer, Court of Justice of Rio Grande do Sul

Apr 2019 — Present

At the department of financial systems, I was the mainly responsible for incidents and software development of Scheduler and Selo. Scheduler is an ETL system for banking integration for payment of expenses, deposits, payment order, marshal payment and others with 40K payments per month, with average of 30M Brazilian reais (5,3M dollars). Selo (the Portuguese word for a notary seal) is a fiscal system for tax collection from private registry office, refund and other legal payment, with 5M stamps/month and 800 users. The main challenge was the lack of documentation and the security issues. My solution was to propose an smart documentation infrastructure (code as infrastructure) for self updating as the system evolved. Also I reported, to security office, 14 issues I found.

Another challenge was unreadable and non abstract code and non normalized database structure, spoiling the potential of intelligence and accuracy of very critical operation because of the problem of bigger payable than receivable. The solution that I propose was the refactoring of the system part by part.

I also worked with the Distribution module of Selo, which is a complex routine that calculates the compensation value for Exempt Services and Minimum Income. In this context, I developed a solution for real-world problem, using mathematics, that nobody else had solve.

Because of Corona Virus pandemic, the job changed to remote and I developed the pair programming culture with new colleagues in this context.
